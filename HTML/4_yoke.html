Survivor's Stories

Real stories of resilience, healing, and community support.

Leilua Lino — From Survivor to Advocate Against Gender‑Based Violence

Who She Is

Leilua was only nine years old when she was raped by her father. Despite the immense trauma, she demonstrated incredible strength by taking her case to court, which ultimately led to her father's imprisonment in 2018 when she was a teenager.

Instead of allowing the violence to silence her, she transformed her experience into a platform, becoming a powerful voice for survivors and tirelessly raising awareness about gender-based violence in her community and globally.

Advocacy & Impact

Leilua actively works with the Samoa Victim Support Group (SVSG), an organization dedicated to helping survivors of abuse. She also serves as an ambassador, committed to raising awareness of gender-based violence and child abuse.

Education and Outreach

Through speeches in schools, community programs, and public forums, Leilua educates others about:

  • Violence prevention
  • The concept of consent
  • Available support systems

Empowering Youth

Her work helps young people know their rights and where to seek help.

Recognition

Her bravery and significant impact have earned her international recognition:

  • She was a finalist for the prestigious International Children’s Peace Prize.
  • She received a Commonwealth award for sustainable development.

Chanel Miller — Reclaiming Identity and Finding Community After Trauma

Who She Is

Chanel Miller is a writer, artist, and advocate who became known internationally after publicly sharing her story as a survivor of sexual assault. Her memoir Know My Name chronicles her healing journey and her process of reclaiming her identity after being known only as “Emily Doe” during her assailant’s criminal trial.

Her Personal Story

After being sexually assaulted while she was a college student, Miller spent years navigating trauma, stigma, and the criminal justice system. Rather than letting the abuse define her life in silence, she chose to reclaim her voice by telling her story on her terms. Her decision to publish Know My Name was an act of claiming back her identity — rejecting the anonymity imposed on her by the legal process, and standing fully in her truth.

Rebuilding with Support and Authenticity

Through writing, speaking out publicly, and engaging with survivor communities, Miller found inclusive spaces where her experiences were validated and supported. By connecting with others who understood her journey, she was able to transform her pain into purpose — helping others feel less alone and encouraging society to take survivors’ voices seriously.

Advocacy, Growth & Impact

  • Her memoir Know My Name became a best‑selling book and received critical acclaim, including major literary awards.
  • Miller has used her platform to speak about trauma, healing, justice, and the importance of centering survivors’ voices in legal reforms and cultural conversations.
  • Her public presence has helped shape how communities think about consent, respect, and the dignity of sexual assault survivors — inspiring others to reclaim their own identities and narratives.
